Vote Poster

One of the assignments in a Digital Design class I took was to create a nonpartisan Vote poster, right around the time of the 2016 U.S. Presidential Election. It took me a few iterations on different ideas before I stumbled upon one that really struck me. Though the issue isn't exactly neutral across all political views, I included both parties in the picture, communicating the urgency of the issue while easing some of the tension through the illustration's subtle absurdity.

Including the patriotic red and blue colors was practically a requirement, but to fit the post-apocalpytic theme, I kept the colors muted and used white to frame the space and highlight the text at the top. If I were to re-do this poster, I would experiment with different styles for the "Vote" lettering; Though the 3D effect does draw attention to the call-to-action, the angle interacts awkwardly with the 2D elements on the page. Instead, I would flatten the letters but use other techniques like contrast or scale for emphasis.
