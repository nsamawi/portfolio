Finding Neema

Create an autobiographical comic of your most embarrassing moment: this was the prompt for the final assignment of a Design classes. While this was probably not my most embarrassing moment, I instantly thought back to an art field trip senior year of high school, when we visited a nature park during the peak of autumn to spend the day painting en plein air.

Though we were specifically instructed NOT to go off on our own, and though I have a terrible sense of direction and, at that time, a potato for a phone and absolutely no cell service in the park, I was so captivated by this gentle waterfall running underneath a bridge that I insisted on sitting there and painting all by myself.

This comic was wildly fun to make – illustrating my classmates and teachers as shrunk-down characters, communicating what happened through dialogue and storyboarding, reflecting the fall color scheme through a limited palette of oranges, yellows, and greens. I even recreated the scene I painted (see below) in several of the frames. Honestly, getting lost was kind of worth it.
